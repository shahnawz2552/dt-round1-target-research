# Fail List — Companies Investigated and Rejected

This list demonstrates the ICP judgment exercised during research. Every company below was a real candidate that *looked* like a Federer fit on first glance — but failed when verified against DT's eligibility gates or auto-disqualifiers. Documenting *why* they fail is as important as documenting why the 25 pass.

## Pattern 1 — PE-controlled (auto-disqualifier per assignment)

The single most common failure pattern in Hyderabad specialty chem/pharma is **PE acquisition of formerly promoter-driven companies**. The assignment explicitly cites Suven Pharma as an example. The full list of recent (last 5 years) PE acquisitions of Federer-looking Hyderabad specialty pharma:

|---------|----------|------|------------------|----------------|
| **Suven Pharma** | Advent International (50.1%) | Dec 2022 | Jasti family | Promoter exit; institutional control. Same example DT cites in the assignment. |
| **Sai Life Sciences** | TPG Capital | 2018 | Krishna Kanumuri | Now PE-controlled CRDMO platform |
| **Aragen Life Sciences (formerly GVK Bio)** | Goldman Sachs (~31%) + Quadria Capital + Avendus FLF III + ChrysCapital exited | Multiple rounds 2021–2025 | GVK Reddy family (33.7%) + Davinder Singh Brar (33.7%) | Promoter holding diluted to <34% across two parties; institutional + ex-Ranbaxy CEO control |
| **RA Chem Pharma** | Advent International (controlling) → merged into **Cohance Lifesciences** | 2020, then Nov 2022 | Originally subsidiary of Micro Labs | Multi-step institutional control |
| **Optimus Pharma / Optimus Drugs** | PAG-led consortium (via **Sekhmet Pharmaventures**, with CX Partners + Samara Capital) | May 2022 | Dr D. Srinivas Reddy | Now PE-platform asset |
| **Avra Laboratories** | Advent International (~$100M) | Feb 2022 | Dr A. V. Rama Rao (founder, ex-IICT scientist) | Even an iconic scientist-founder exited to PE |
| **Symed Labs** | Carlyle-backed Viyash Lifesciences (now Viyash Scientific) | June 2021 | Mohan Rao Dodda (founder) | Amalgamated into Viyash group |

**What this teaches us about the ICP:** any Hyderabad CDMO/specialty-pharma at the Rs.300Cr-Rs.1,000Cr scale with USFDA approvals is statistically likely to have already been acquired or be in active acquisition discussions. The Federer-fit pool sits **either** below the PE radar (Rs.50–250Cr) **or** in segments PE has not yet targeted (antisera, fermentation APIs, niche injectables, novel device categories).

## What the fail list teaches us about Hyderabad specialty chem/biotech

1. **PE has eaten ~40% of the obvious mid-cap Federer pool in the last 5 years.** Any Hyderabad CDMO/specialty pharma that crossed Rs.250Cr post-2018 is statistically more likely to be PE-owned than not.
2. **The next-decile size companies have outgrown the band.** Apollo Micro, Virupaksha Organics, MTAR Technologies — all have crossed Rs.500Cr in the last 18 months. Without recency-checking, a pipeline would be polluted with stale Federer profiles.
3. **The remaining ICP pool is bimodal:** either (a) sub-Rs.300Cr USFDA-cleared specialty CDMOs that haven't shown up on PE radars yet (Honour Lab, Lee Pharma, Maithri Drugs profile), or (b) niche-segment specialists where PE doesn't have a thesis yet (Vins Bioproducts on antisera, Krebs on fermentation APIs, Jodas on sterile injectables).
4. **Family succession is the most under-disclosed factor.** Many Hyderabad specialty pharma founders are 1980s/1990s-vintage; gen-2 transitions are happening but rarely reflected in MCA director master data quickly. C8 should be probed in the discovery call rather than scored confidently from public data.

This insight directly informs Part B Question 2's 1,000-company proposal — particularly the **PE re-screen at Day 28** and the **revenue boundary re-pull** components of the QA architecture.
